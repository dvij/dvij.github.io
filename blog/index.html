A Map of Tractable QCQPs

optimization
theory
semidefinite-programming
Characterizing when SDP relaxations of quadratically constrained quadratic programs are exact — a tour through the S-lemma, chordal sparsity, sign definiteness, and their interplay.
Author

Dj

Published

February 23, 2026

Quadratically constrained quadratic programs (QCQPs) are NP-hard in general, yet a surprising number of practically important instances admit polynomial-time exact solutions via semidefinite programming (SDP) relaxation. The goal of this post is to build a map of the tractable territory — collecting the tightest known results and showing how the key conditions fit together.

Problem Setup

A QCQP takes the form:

\min_{x \in \mathbb{R}^n} \; x^\top A_0 x + 2b_0^\top x + c_0 \quad \text{s.t.} \quad x^\top A_i x + 2b_i^\top x + c_i \leq 0, \quad i = 1, \ldots, m

where A_i \in \mathbb{S}^n, b_i \in \mathbb{R}^n, c_i \in \mathbb{R}. When all A_i \succeq 0 this is a convex QP; the interesting regime is when some A_i are indefinite.

The Canonical SDP Relaxation

Introduce X = xx^\top and relax the rank-1 constraint to X \succeq 0:

\min \; \langle \hat{A}_0, \hat{X} \rangle \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \langle \hat{A}_i, \hat{X} \rangle \leq 0, \quad \hat{X}_{00} = 1, \quad \hat{X} \succeq 0

where \hat{A}_i = \begin{pmatrix} c_i & b_i^\top \\ b_i & A_i \end{pmatrix} is the (n+1) \times (n+1) lifted matrix.

The SDP relaxation is exact when the relaxed optimal value equals the QCQP optimal value, and a rank-1 optimal \hat{X} can be recovered (equivalently, zero duality gap plus rank-1 recoverability).

The central question: when does this happen?

The Three Pillars of Tractability

The known tractable cases rest on three structural conditions that can be combined.

Pillar 1: Few Constraints — The S-Lemma

The most fundamental result is the S-lemma (Yakubovich 1971).

Note

Theorem (S-Lemma). Let f, g be quadratics with \exists \bar{x}: g(\bar{x}) < 0. Then f(x) \geq 0 \;\forall x: g(x) \leq 0 \iff \exists \lambda \geq 0:\; f(x) - \lambda g(x) \geq 0 \;\forall x.

The consequence for QCQPs: when m = 1, the SDP relaxation is always exact (under Slater’s condition). The dual multiplier \lambda certifies zero duality gap, and rank-1 recovery follows from the rank characterization of SDP optima.

This fails for m = 2 in general — counterexamples exist already in \mathbb{R}^2.

Extensions:

  • Equality constraint: the S-lemma holds with \lambda unrestricted, covering the trust-region subproblem \min\{x^\top A_0 x + 2b_0^\top x : \|x\|^2 \leq 1\}. This is the workhorse of trust-region optimization methods and is solvable in near-linear time.
  • CDT (two-ball) problem: for m = 2 ball constraints, exactness holds under mild nondegeneracy (Ye & Zhang 2003).
  • Complex domain: over \mathbb{C}^n, the SDP relaxation is exact for m \leq 2 with no additional assumptions, and for m \leq 3 rank-1 constraints (So 2010). The complex case is uniformly easier.

The Barvinok-Pataki bound gives intuition for why few constraints help: a rank-r solution to the lifted SDP exists whenever r(r+1)/2 \leq m+1, so small m forces low-rank solutions and makes rank-1 recovery feasible.

Pillar 2: Sign Definiteness of the Lagrangian

The deepest unifying condition is joint sign definiteness.

Definition. The QCQP is jointly definite if \exists \lambda \geq 0 such that A_0 + \sum_i \lambda_i A_i \succeq 0.

In words: the dual multipliers can convexify the Lagrangian. This is exactly the condition that makes the saddle-point structure of the Lagrangian well-behaved.

Important

Theorem (Burer 2012; sharpened by Burer & Ye 2020). If the Lagrangian Hessian at the optimum has at most one negative eigenvalue, the SDP relaxation is exact.

This subsumes several classical results:

Condition Why it implies \leq 1 neg. eigenvalue
Convex QCQP (A_i \succeq 0) Lagrangian is PSD, zero negative eigenvalues
TRS / single constraint S-lemma gives \lambda^* with PSD Lagrangian
Joint definiteness By definition
A_i simultaneously diagonalizable (Huang & Zhang 2007) Diagonalize and apply 1D argument

The “at most one negative eigenvalue” condition is tight — problems with two or more negative eigenvalues can be NP-hard.

Pillar 3: Chordal Sparsity

Chordal structure is the decomposition engine that makes large-scale tractability possible.

Setup. Associate a graph G = (V, E) to the QCQP where edges correspond to nonzero off-diagonal blocks of the A_i. If G is chordal (every cycle of length \geq 4 has a chord), its maximal cliques \{C_1, \ldots, C_k\} form a perfect elimination ordering.

Note

Theorem (Kim & Kojima 2003; Vandenberghe & Andersen 2015). If G is chordal, the global SDP decomposes into clique-local SDPs: X \succeq 0 \iff X_{C_\ell} \succeq 0 \;\forall \ell, \quad \text{(plus consistency constraints)}

Why this matters: if each clique subproblem is tractable (via Pillars 1 or 2), the whole problem is tractable — with complexity polynomial in n for fixed treewidth \omega.

This is the standard approach in power systems (ACOPF), sensor network localization, and polynomial optimization via the Lasserre/SOS hierarchy. Sparse moment matrices decompose along the chordal structure of the variable interaction graph.

The real power is in combinations: a problem that is neither globally few-constrained nor globally sparse may still be tractable if it has favorable local structure within each chordal clique.

Open Directions

A few places where the map has genuine gaps:

The real two-constraint case. The CDT (two-ball) problem is tractable, but the general 2-constraint non-convex QCQP over \mathbb{R}^n is not fully resolved — unlike the complex case where m \leq 2 always works.

Beyond chordal. Graphs with bounded genus or excluded minors have partial results but no clean tractability boundary.

Aggregation + sparsity. Combining S-lemma style reasoning (few constraints) with chordal decomposition (few variables per clique) is the most powerful known paradigm, but the interaction between the two is not fully characterized.

SOS hierarchy convergence rates. Lasserre/moment hierarchies converge for compact feasible sets, but the level at which exactness is achieved for a given QCQP depends on algebraic degree in ways that aren’t fully understood.

Copositive relaxations. Every non-convex QCQP over the nonnegative orthant has an exact copositive reformulation, but the gap between the tractable doubly-nonnegative (DNN) inner approximation and the true copositive cone is an active area.
